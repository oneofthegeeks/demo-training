# Scenario 5: "We're Opening a Second Location"
**Complexity:** Advanced
**GoTo Connect Features:** Multi-location setup, per-location numbers and auto-attendants, overflow call routing, user management across locations, admin portal

---

## Business Context

Shear Bliss has been running well. Taylor has gotten the phone system working — the auto-attendant, business SMS, reporting, and her team on the app. Now she's expanding: she's signing a lease on a second location across town, called **Shear Bliss Westside**, opening in about 10 weeks. She's hiring two new stylists for that location, and Jenna (her lead stylist) will split time between both. Taylor wants to manage the whole business without being physically at both places every day. She's nervous about the complexity — right now the phone system is dialed in, and she doesn't want to break what's working while adding something new.

**Taylor's mindset going in:** Excited about growth but anxious about operational complexity. She wants to feel like the system will scale with her, not become another thing to manage.

---

## Pain Points to Surface

1. How do clients reach the right location? (Two locations, potentially confusing)
2. What happens when one location is overwhelmed with calls — can they overflow to the other?
3. Taylor can't be in two places — she needs visibility and control across both from wherever she is
4. Jenna floats between locations — how does her phone setup handle that?
5. Taylor wants one bill, one admin login, one system — not two separate setups

---

## Sample Dialogue

> **Note for SC:** Taylor is a seasoned GoTo user now. She understands the platform. This conversation is peer-to-peer — she'll ask sharper questions and she'll think about edge cases. Treat her as an informed buyer making a scaling decision.

---

**SC:** Congratulations on Westside — that's a big step. Tell me where your head is on the phone setup for the new location.

**Taylor:** Okay so I have a few worries. First — do I need a whole separate account? Like, do I pay for everything twice?

**SC:** No. We add Westside to your existing GoTo account. One admin login, one billing relationship, two locations. *(shows multi-location account structure)* You manage both from the same place you manage Shear Bliss today.

**Taylor:** Okay good. So Westside gets its own number?

**SC:** Yes — its own number, its own auto-attendant, its own call routing. *(shows second location setup)* A client who's a Westside regular calls the Westside number and gets "Thank you for calling Shear Bliss Westside" — separate from the original location. They never get confused about which location they've reached.

**Taylor:** What if I want to run a promotion and tell everyone to call one main number? Can I do that?

**SC:** Yes. *(shows call routing options)* You can set up a main Shear Bliss number that routes based on what the caller presses — "Press 1 for our original location, press 2 for Westside" — or you can keep them completely separate and let clients dial the location directly. Your call.

**Taylor:** I think I'd want them separate to start. Now — what about overflow? Like, Westside is new. What if they get slammed one day and can't answer all the calls?

**SC:** Great question. This is overflow routing. *(shows overflow configuration)* You can set it so that if a Westside call goes unanswered after, say, 20 seconds, it automatically rolls over to the original location's ring group. The caller doesn't know. They just get answered.

**Taylor:** And my original team would know it's a Westside call?

**SC:** Yes — the app shows them it's coming from the Westside queue. *(shows call label on app)* So they can greet appropriately: "Thanks for calling Shear Bliss Westside, how can I help you?"

**Taylor:** That's really smart. Okay — Jenna. She's going to be at both locations. How does that work for her?

**SC:** Jenna stays on one user account — her GoTo profile. *(shows user management panel)* We assign her to both locations. *(clicks to assign)* Now she can receive calls from either location's queue, depending on where she is and whether she's set herself as available. She doesn't need two phones, two apps, two anything.

**Taylor:** Can she switch which location she's "at" in the app?

**SC:** Yes — she sets her status and her location in the app. *(shows status/location toggle)* When she's at Westside, she's in the Westside queue. When she's back at the original location, she flips it. She's always only getting calls relevant to where she is.

**Taylor:** And I can see all of this from my phone?

**SC:** Let me show you the admin portal. *(shows dashboard)* This is your view. You can see: calls coming into both locations right now, who's available at each location, the queue status, missed calls broken down by location. *(shows location-level reporting)* You have one dashboard that shows you the health of both locations — from wherever you are.

**Taylor:** So I could be at home on a Sunday and know what's happening at both salons.

**SC:** Exactly. You're not dependent on someone texting you to tell you it's busy. You have eyes on both locations from your phone.

**Taylor:** *(pauses)* Okay. I was worried this was going to get complicated. This actually feels manageable.

**SC:** That's the point. You built a system that works — we're just extending it. Same platform, same app, same admin login. Westside inherits everything you've already figured out.

## Features Demoed in This Scenario

- **Multi-location setup** — separate numbers, auto-attendants, and routing per location under one account
- **Overflow routing** — calls that go unanswered at one location automatically roll to the other
- **Cross-location user management** — staff assigned to one or both locations, status-based availability
- **Admin portal** — single dashboard with visibility across both locations
- **Per-location reporting** — call volume, missed calls, and queue status broken down by location

## Trainer Notes

**What good looks like:**
- SC positions this as an extension of what Taylor already has — growth, not disruption
- SC answers the billing question first, before Taylor even finishes asking it — show that you anticipated her concern
- The Jenna use case is the emotional heart of this demo — SC should spend real time on it, not rush past it
- "You're not dependent on someone texting you" — this line positions GoTo as giving Taylor back control. Make sure it lands.
- SC ends by reinforcing continuity: *"same platform, same app, same admin login"* — Taylor's instinct to worry about complexity should be directly addressed

**Common SC mistakes to watch for:**
- Over-explaining the technical architecture of multi-location (PSTN routing, SIP trunks, etc.) — Taylor doesn't need this
- Not asking what Taylor wants before configuring — even in this advanced scenario, the demo should feel like a configuration conversation
- Skipping the overflow routing feature — it's a differentiator that small businesses rarely expect and are always impressed by

**Optional objections for trainer use:**

**Objection 1 — Closing a location:**
Taylor says: *"What if I decide Westside isn't working out and I want to close it? What happens to the second location setup?"* — Good SC response: removing a location is straightforward from the admin portal. The Westside number can be released or ported out if Taylor wants to keep it. The original Shear Bliss location and account are completely unaffected. The platform is designed for flexibility — businesses grow and change.

**Objection 2 — Reliability / "what if it goes down?":**
Taylor says: *"This all sounds great, but I'm nervous about depending on an internet-based phone system. What happens if the internet goes down at one of my locations?"* — Good SC response: this is a fair concern for any cloud-based system. GoTo Connect has redundant infrastructure and publishes uptime stats (typically 99.99% uptime). If the internet at one location goes down, calls can be rerouted to the other location or to staff mobile numbers automatically. The mobile app also means staff can take calls over cellular data, not just WiFi, so the salon is never fully unreachable.

**Objection 3 — Managing two bills / vendors:**
Taylor says: *"I already feel like I'm managing a million vendors. Will this mean another bill, another support line, another login?"* — Good SC response: both locations are under one GoTo Connect account — one bill, one admin portal, one support relationship. Taylor is adding a location to her existing system, not a second system. This is exactly the consolidation argument for GoTo Connect: one platform handles the full communication stack for both salons.
