# GoTo Connect Demo Scenarios — Design Spec
**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Author:** Taylor Brinton
**Purpose:** Training material for Solution Consultants learning to demo GoTo Connect

---

## Overview

Five roleplay demo scenarios for Solution Consultant (SC) training. Each scenario places the SC in a live demo/discovery call with Taylor, the owner of a small salon. Taylor plays the customer. Scenarios progress from basic to advanced, each anchored in a real business pain point that the SC must uncover through conversation before demoing the GoTo Connect solution.

---

## Customer Profile: Taylor

- **Business:** Small salon, 6 staff (mix of employees and booth renters)
- **Current phone setup:** No business phone system — all staff use personal cell phones
- **How clients interact:** Calling to book/change appointments, calling with questions (hours, services, pricing), texting
- **Core pain points:**
  - Clients can't reliably reach the salon
  - Calls go to personal voicemail or get ignored
  - No visibility into missed calls or booking opportunities lost
  - Staff don't want to give out personal numbers
  - No professional image (no greeting, hold music, auto-attendant)
  - No way to manage after-hours calls
  - Clients text stylists directly on personal phones, blurring boundaries

---

## Scenario Structure

Each scenario follows this format:
1. **Business Context** — What Taylor's situation looks like going into this call
2. **Pain Points to Surface** — What the SC should uncover through discovery questions
3. **GoTo Connect Features to Demo** — What to show once the pain is established
4. **Success Criteria** — What a good SC demo looks like for this scenario

---

## The 5 Scenarios

### Scenario 1 — "We Just Use Our Cell Phones"
**Complexity:** Basic

**Business Context:**
Taylor owns a 6-person salon. There is no business phone number. Clients call whoever's number they have — sometimes Taylor, sometimes a stylist. When Taylor is with a client, she lets it go to his personal voicemail. He hasn't thought much about it until bookings started feeling inconsistent.

**Pain Points to Surface:**
- No dedicated business number
- Clients reaching different people (or no one) depending on who they call
- Personal voicemail answering for the business — no professional greeting
- Taylor doesn't know what he's missing when he's hands-on with clients

**GoTo Connect Features to Demo:**
- Dedicated business phone number
- GoTo Connect mobile app (staff can use their personal phones without giving out personal numbers)
- Basic voicemail with a professional recorded greeting

**Success Criteria:**
SC uncovers the "personal cell phone" situation through discovery before jumping to demo. Demo shows how GoTo Connect gives the salon a real business identity on the phone without requiring new hardware.

---

### Scenario 2 — "I Don't Know How Many Calls We Miss"
**Complexity:** Basic–Intermediate

**Business Context:**
Taylor has had a dedicated business number for a little while (or is considering it). He suspects the salon is losing bookings to missed calls but has no data. He doesn't know when the salon is busiest on the phones, whether voicemails are being checked, or how often calls go unanswered.

**Pain Points to Surface:**
- Zero visibility into call volume or missed calls
- Voicemails may be going unheard — no notification system
- No way to identify peak call times to staff accordingly
- Gut feeling of lost revenue but no proof

**GoTo Connect Features to Demo:**
- Call reporting and analytics dashboard
- Missed call log
- Voicemail-to-email (voicemail transcribed and emailed so nothing is missed)

**Success Criteria:**
SC connects the feature demo directly to Taylor's fear of lost bookings. Reporting is shown as a business intelligence tool, not just a phone feature.

---

### Scenario 3 — "Clients Keep Texting Our Personal Numbers"
**Complexity:** Intermediate

**Business Context:**
Clients have gotten comfortable texting stylists directly on their personal phones to book, cancel, or ask questions. Stylists are frustrated — they get texts at 10pm, they feel obligated to respond, and Taylor has no visibility into any of it. One stylist has started leaving client messages on read and Taylor is worried about the client experience.

**Pain Points to Surface:**
- Staff/client boundaries being crossed via personal texting
- Taylor has no oversight of client communication happening off-platform
- Inconsistent client experience depending on which stylist responds (or doesn't)
- Staff morale issue around being reachable 24/7 on personal devices

**GoTo Connect Features to Demo:**
- Business SMS on the salon's shared number
- Team inbox for SMS (all texts come to one place the team manages together)
- Separation of personal and business communication on the mobile app

**Success Criteria:**
SC surfaces the staff frustration and client experience angle before demoing SMS. Demo shows business texting as a boundary-setting and professionalism tool, not just a feature.

---

### Scenario 4 — "We Need to Look More Professional"
**Complexity:** Intermediate–Advanced

**Business Context:**
A competing salon opened nearby and Taylor has noticed they have a much more polished phone experience — callers are greeted professionally, can press options to reach the right person, and get a proper after-hours message. Taylor's callers still just get a cell phone ringing or going to personal voicemail. He wants to upgrade the experience.

**Pain Points to Surface:**
- No auto-attendant or IVR — callers don't know who to ask for or what to press
- After-hours calls ring forever or hit personal voicemail
- No on-hold music or experience while clients wait
- The salon doesn't feel like a real business on the phone

**GoTo Connect Features to Demo:**
- Auto-attendant / IVR ("Press 1 to book an appointment, Press 2 for hours and services")
- Custom hold music and professional greeting recording
- After-hours call routing (send to voicemail, play a message, route differently)
- Business hours configuration

**Success Criteria:**
SC uses the competitor comparison as a motivator without dismissing Taylor's current setup. Demo shows the full "professional phone experience" end-to-end — a caller's journey from ring to resolution.

---

### Scenario 5 — "We're Opening a Second Location"
**Complexity:** Advanced

**Business Context:**
Taylor is expanding — opening a second salon location across town in the next 3 months. He's worried about how to manage phone calls across two locations, how to make sure clients reach the right salon, and how she (as owner) can keep visibility across both without being on-site all the time. Some senior stylists will work at both locations.

**Pain Points to Surface:**
- How do clients reach the right location?
- What happens when one location is busy — can calls roll to the other?
- Taylor wants to manage both locations but not be on-site constantly
- Staff who float between locations need to be reachable at either
- Reporting needs to cover both locations to understand business health

**GoTo Connect Features to Demo:**
- Multi-location setup (separate numbers, separate auto-attendants per location)
- Call routing between locations (overflow routing if one location is busy)
- User management (assigning staff to one or both locations)
- Admin portal — managing everything from one dashboard
- Reporting across both locations

**Success Criteria:**
SC treats this as a growth/expansion conversation, not just a phone upgrade. Demo positions GoTo Connect as scalable infrastructure that grows with the business. Admin portal is shown as Taylor's control center across both locations.

---

## Training Notes for SCs

- **Discovery before demo:** In every scenario, the SC should ask questions to surface the pain before showing any feature. Jumping straight to the product demo is a red flag.
- **Taylor's persona:** Taylor is friendly but not technical. He doesn't know VoIP terminology. Avoid jargon — translate features into business outcomes.
- **Escalation path:** Each scenario builds on the previous. SCs should be able to run Scenario 1 alone or run all 5 as a progression showing a customer's full journey.
- **Objection opportunities:** Scenarios 3 and 4 are good places for trainers to introduce objections (cost, complexity, change management) for SCs to handle.
