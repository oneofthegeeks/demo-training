Scenario 2 · Basic–Intermediate

"I Don't Know How Many Calls We Miss"

Taylor has a business number but zero visibility into what's happening on it.

Basic–Intermediate
Business Context

Taylor has been using a business phone number for a few months — a step in the right direction — but the setup is still basic. Calls ring to his cell, he checks voicemail manually when he remembers, and he has no reporting or visibility into what's happening on the phones. He's had a few slow weeks and is starting to wonder if there's a pattern he's missing. A friend who owns a restaurant mentioned they use their phone system to see "when people are trying to reach them most" — and Taylor is curious whether that's something he could do too.

Taylor's mindset going in: Slightly anxious. He suspects he's losing bookings but has no way to prove it. He's open to being shown data and loves the idea of understanding his business better.

Pain Points to Surface
Trainer — Surface these through questions, not statements
  1. No visibility into how many calls come in vs. how many are answered
  2. Voicemails sit in the system unheard — no notification when one arrives
  3. No way to identify peak call times to make sure the right staff are available
  4. Taylor's gut says he's losing bookings — but he has no data to confirm or act on it
Sample Dialogue
This scenario is about turning anxiety into clarity. Taylor already suspects there's a problem — your job is to help him see it clearly.
SC
How's the phone situation been since you got the business number set up?
Taylor
Better, I think. At least clients aren't calling five different numbers anymore. But honestly... I'm not totally sure it's working like it should.
SC
What makes you say that?
Taylor
We had a really slow couple of weeks in January. And I kept thinking — are people calling and not getting through? Or is it just a slow time of year? I genuinely don't know.
SC
That uncertainty is actually really common. Can I ask — right now, if I told you that 30 calls came in last Tuesday, how many of them do you think were answered?
Taylor
(pauses) I have no idea. I couldn't even tell you if 30 calls came in.
SC
Right. You're running the business completely blind on the phone side. What do you do with voicemails right now?
Taylor
I check them when I remember. Honestly sometimes it's end of day before I think about it.
SC
So someone could call at 10am, leave a message saying they want to book for Saturday, and you might not hear it until 6pm?
Taylor
Yeah. Which is terrible, I know.
SC
It's not terrible — it's just a gap in the system. Have you ever called someone back from a voicemail and they'd already gone somewhere else?
Taylor
(sighs) Yes. More than once.
SC
So there are two things happening: calls you probably don't know you're missing, and voicemails you're hearing too late to act on. What would it change for you if you could see both?
Taylor
I mean, that would be huge. I'd actually know if January was a fluke or if we have a real problem. And I could stop worrying about it constantly.
SC
Let me show you what that looks like.
SC transitions to demo
SC
This is the reporting dashboard in GoTo Connect. (shows dashboard) Every call that comes into Shear Bliss — answered, missed, sent to voicemail — shows up here. So right now you can see: this week, you had 47 calls come in. 31 were answered. 16 were missed.
Taylor
16 missed calls in a week?
SC
That's what the data would show you. You can filter by day, by hour, by which team member. (shows time-of-day breakdown) You see this spike here on Monday mornings? That's probably people calling to book for the week. Is anyone specifically available to take calls Monday mornings?
Taylor
No, that's actually when we do our staff meeting. Nobody's really by their phone.
SC
So you're losing calls right at your peak window — and you didn't know because there was nothing to look at. Now you have a reason to adjust. (shows missed call log) And this is your missed call log — every number that called and didn't get through, timestamped. You can call them back directly from here.
Taylor
Oh wow. So I could actually work through that list at the end of the day?
SC
Exactly. And this is voicemail-to-email. (shows setting) Every voicemail that comes in gets emailed to you — transcribed, so you can read it without listening — with the caller's number. You don't have to remember to check voicemail. It comes to you.
Taylor
That alone would save me. I'm terrible at remembering to check it.
SC
Most people are. The point is — nothing falls through the cracks. You see every attempt, every message, and you can respond fast enough that clients don't go down the street.
Features Demoed
  • Call reporting dashboard — total calls in, answered, missed; filterable by day/time/user
  • Missed call log — timestamped list of every unanswered call with callback option
  • Voicemail-to-email — voicemails transcribed and emailed automatically
Trainer Notes

What Good Looks Like

  • SC surfaces the "blind spot" through questions before pulling up reporting
  • SC ties the missed Monday morning calls back to the staff meeting — making the insight specific and actionable
  • Demo is framed around business outcomes (recovering lost bookings) not features

Common SC Mistakes to Watch For

  • Jumping straight to the dashboard without establishing that Taylor doesn't know what he's missing
  • Showing every report available instead of focusing on the 2–3 that matter to Taylor
  • Not asking "what would it change for you" before the demo — that question sets up the value

Optional Objections for Trainer Use

Objection 1 Notification overload

Taylor says: "This is great — but I already have a ton of notifications coming at me. I don't need more emails."

Good SC response: Voicemail-to-email is configurable — Taylor can choose who receives the notification (just him, or also the receptionist), and the transcription means he can scan it in 5 seconds rather than listening to a 90-second voicemail. It's designed to reduce effort, not add noise.

Objection 2 Contract / commitment fear

Taylor says: "I'm interested, but I don't want to be locked into a long contract. What if my business changes?"

Good SC response: Address the contract terms directly and honestly (month-to-month options vs. annual, what happens if they need to add or remove users). Reinforce that the platform scales up and down — adding a new stylist or removing a booth renter is a single change in the admin portal, not a new contract negotiation.

Objection 3 "My carrier already does this"

Taylor says: "Wait — doesn't my phone carrier already give me something like this? I feel like I saw something about a business line add-on."

Good SC response: Acknowledge that carriers offer basic call forwarding add-ons, then differentiate: carrier add-ons are single-line and have no reporting, no team inbox, no auto-attendant, no SMS, and no admin visibility. GoTo Connect is a business communication platform — the reporting alone shows Taylor things his carrier will never surface.
