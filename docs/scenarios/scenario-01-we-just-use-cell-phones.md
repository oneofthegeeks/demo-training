# Scenario 1: "We Just Use Our Cell Phones"
**Complexity:** Basic
**GoTo Connect Features:** Business number, mobile app, professional voicemail greeting

---

## Business Context

Taylor is the owner of a 6-person salon called **Shear Bliss**. He has two full-time stylists, two booth renters, and a part-time receptionist who only works weekends. There is no business phone number. Clients call whoever's number they have — sometimes Taylor, sometimes one of the stylists. When Taylor is with a client, she sends calls to voicemail — his personal voicemail with no business greeting. He's noticed that some weeks feel slow and wonders if they're losing bookings, but he hasn't connected that to the phone situation yet.

**Taylor's mindset going in:** He's open to hearing about options but doesn't feel urgent pain. He thinks the phone situation is "fine" — it's just how they've always done it.

---

| | |
|---|---|

| **Tech comfort level** | Moderate — uses apps daily, not technical |

---

## Pain Points to Surface

The SC should uncover these through questions — not state them upfront:

1. No dedicated business phone number (clients have different numbers for different people)
2. Calls going to Taylor's personal voicemail with no business greeting
3. Staff reluctance to give out personal numbers
4. No way to know what's being missed when Taylor is hands-on with clients

---

## Sample Dialogue

> **Note for SC:** Your goal in this scenario is to uncover the phone situation through natural conversation before showing any product. Resist the urge to start demoing immediately.

---

**SC:** Thanks for making time today, Taylor. Before I show you anything, I'd love to understand how your salon operates today — just so I can make sure what I show you is actually relevant to you. Sound okay?

**Taylor:** Sure, yeah. I'm mostly curious what GoTo Connect even is. My friend mentioned it but I don't really know much about phone systems.

**SC:** Totally fair — we'll get there. Let's start simple: right now, if a client wants to call Shear Bliss to book an appointment, what number do they call?

**Taylor:** *(pauses)* Honestly… it depends. Some clients have my number, some have my stylist Jenna's number. I put my cell on the website a while back so some people just call me directly.

**SC:** Got it. And when you're in the middle of a haircut — which I'm guessing is most of the day — what happens to those calls?

**Taylor:** I mean, I just let it go to voicemail. I try to call back when I have a break.

**SC:** And what does your voicemail say right now?

**Taylor:** *(laughs)* Just my personal voicemail. "Hey, you've reached Taylor, leave a message." Nothing fancy.

**SC:** So someone calling to book at a salon hears a generic personal voicemail. Do you think they leave a message, or do they just hang up?

**Taylor:** *(thinks)* Probably hang up. I would. Or they'd try someone else.

**SC:** That's a really honest answer. Have you ever had a client tell you they tried calling and couldn't reach anyone?

**Taylor:** Yeah, actually. A couple of times. One person said she just booked at the place down the street because nobody answered.

**SC:** That's the moment right there. *(pause)* What if I told you that for most salons running this way, there's a gap between how many people try to reach them and how many actually get through — and they have no way to see it?

**Taylor:** That's kind of scary honestly. I always figured people would just try again.

**SC:** Some do. But a lot don't — especially for something like a salon where there are other options nearby. Can I show you what it looks like when a small business has a real phone presence, even without any desk phones or new hardware?

**Taylor:** Yeah, definitely.

---

*[SC transitions to demo]*

**SC:** So the first thing GoTo Connect gives you is a real business phone number — one number that represents Shear Bliss, not Taylor's personal cell. *(shows number setup in platform)* Your clients call that number, and it rings on your phone — but through the GoTo app, so it shows up as a business call, not your personal number.

**Taylor:** Wait — so it rings on my same phone?

**SC:** Exactly the same phone you have now. No desk phones, no new equipment. You just download the app. *(shows mobile app)* You answer business calls through here, your personal calls come in normally. A client never sees your cell number, and you always know when it's a business call coming in.

**Taylor:** Oh that's actually really smart. What about Jenna? She hates giving out her personal number to clients.

**SC:** Jenna gets her own account on the same system. She can take calls through the app too, same way. *(shows user setup)* Every one of your staff can be on the same business number — calls can go to whoever's available.

**Taylor:** And voicemail?

**SC:** You record a custom greeting. So instead of "Hey, you've reached Taylor"... *(plays example greeting)* ...callers hear "Thank you for calling Shear Bliss Salon. We're with a client right now — please leave your name and number and we'll call you right back." Professional, on-brand, every time.

**Taylor:** That sounds way better than what we have now. *(laughs)*

## Trainer Notes

**What good looks like:**
- SC asks open questions and lets Taylor reveal the personal cell phone situation naturally
- SC uses Taylor's "place down the street" comment as a pivot to urgency
- Demo is positioned as "you don't need new hardware" — removing the biggest assumed objection

**Common SC mistakes to watch for:**
- Leading with features before understanding the current setup
- Using jargon ("VoIP," "hosted PBX," "softphone") — Taylor doesn't know these terms
- Demoing too fast — Taylor needs a moment to process each piece

**Optional objections for trainer use:**

**Objection 1 — Price before value:**
Taylor asks: *"How much does this cost?"* — Use this early in the demo, before the full value has been established. Good SC response: acknowledge the question, give a ballpark range, then redirect — *"Before we go deep on pricing, let me make sure this is even the right fit for you. Can I show you what this would actually look like for Shear Bliss first?"*

**Objection 2 — Number porting concern:**
Taylor asks: *"I've had my cell number on the salon website for two years. If I switch to GoTo Connect, do I lose that number?"* — Good SC response: explain number porting — Taylor can keep his existing number and have it transferred to GoTo Connect. The process typically takes 2–4 weeks and runs in parallel so there's no downtime. He doesn't have to start over.

**Objection 3 — Staff adoption resistance:**
Taylor says: *"Honestly, I'm not sure my team will actually download another app. They're already annoyed when I ask them to use new things."* — Good SC response: acknowledge it's real, note the app is lightweight and most users are up and running in under 5 minutes, and point out the personal benefit to staff — they stop getting client calls on their personal number the moment they're on the app. The incentive is self-motivated.
